Who We Are

Nestora began with a single shelf

In 2012, founder Elena Marsh was styling her Chicago apartment and found herself frustrated. Everything either felt too expensive, too generic, or too mass-produced. She wanted pieces that felt handmade, considered, and real.

So she started sourcing them herself — from small studios in Portugal, local ceramicists in Vermont, weavers in Colombia, and craftspeople across the US. She sold them first to friends, then to strangers, and Nestora was born.

Today we work with over 80 independent makers across 24 countries. Every piece in our collection is selected by our small in-house team against a strict set of criteria: quality of materials, integrity of craft, and lasting design appeal.

We're not a trend shop. We're a home for the things that endure.

What Drives Us

Our Values

Craftsmanship Over Volume

We prioritize depth over breadth. A smaller, better collection beats a large, mediocre one every time. Every piece earns its place through quality and design integrity.

Sustainability as Standard

We favor natural, renewable, and recycled materials wherever possible. Our packaging is fully compostable and we offset 100% of shipping-related carbon emissions.

Fair Maker Relationships

We pay our makers fairly and build long-term partnerships. Many of our artisan relationships span a decade or more. When they thrive, we thrive.

Enduring Design

We don't follow seasonal trends. We select pieces that will still feel right five, ten, twenty years from now. Timeless is our ultimate filter.
